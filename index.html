PHANTOM RECALL

January 2026 ARR Submission

The Problem

Large language models (LLMs) often achieve high performance on standard reasoning benchmarks. However, this performance is frequently a result of pattern matching or memorizing specific puzzle templates from training data—a phenomenon we term Phantom Recall.

Methodology

We introduced a benchmark of well-known logic puzzles paired with semantic perturbations. These perturbations maintain the surface-level details of the "classic" puzzle but change the underlying logic, requiring a different answer. If a model fails by providing the answer to the classic version, it demonstrates a failure of reasoning in favor of memorization.

Core Results

  • Confident Failure: Models output standard solutions even when prompt modifications make them logically impossible.
  • Reasoning Gap: While "Think" modes (e.g., GPT-o1) improve baseline accuracy, robustness against perturbations remains low.
  • Human Agreement: Developed an automated step-level error taxonomy that aligns with human judgment at a 96% rate.

MONEYBALL WITH LLMS

January 2026 ARR Submission

The SporTabSet Benchmark

Tabular summarization in long-context environments (like sports play-by-play) is a major challenge for LLMs. We introduced SPORTABSET, a diagnostic benchmark for event-to-tuple extraction in cricket and basketball narratives.

Research Findings

  • Entity Interference: Errors in long-context summarization are rarely due to poor math; they are caused by "interference," where the model confuses actions between different players or teams.
  • Decomposition: We systematically evaluated decomposition-based strategies (Step 1: Extraction, Step 2: Aggregation). Results show that while these pipelines help, models remain highly sensitive to "surface-level" phrasing changes.
  • Numeric Fidelity: Proved that model accuracy in sports tracking stems from better extraction of intermediate representations rather than an inherent improvement in global arithmetic capabilities.
