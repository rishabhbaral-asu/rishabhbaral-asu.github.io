Research

PHANTOM RECALL

Robustness in Applied NLP

Abstract

Large language models often achieve high scores on classic logic puzzles, but we investigate if they are truly reasoning or simply replaying memorized scripts. PHANTOM RECALL is a benchmark of puzzles paired with perturbations that maintain surface-level details but change logic templates.

  • Exposed memorization vs. true reasoning through Template-Perturbation frameworks across 8 LLM families.
  • Developed an automated logical-equivalence judge and a step-level error taxonomy achieving 96% human agreement.
  • Proved that "Think" modes improve accuracy significantly, but robustness remains a critical unresolved gap.

MONEYBALL WITH LLMS

Long-Context Applied NLP

SporTabSet Benchmark

Introduced a diagnostic benchmark for event-to-tuple extraction in sports play-by-play narratives, requiring models to maintain state over evolving, 10k+ token contexts.

  • Entity Interference: Discovered that failures in long-context summarization stem from "interference" (confusing player identities) rather than arithmetic errors.
  • Decomposition Evaluation: Systematically evaluated extraction vs. aggregation strategies.
  • Numerical Fidelity: Proved that performance gains come from better dissection of the narrative rather than improved global arithmetic.

TEMPORAL PERCEPTION

Cross-Domain Perception in Adverse Weather

Research Objective

Autonomous vehicle perception fails in "Whiteout" (snow/fog) conditions. This project targets a 5-10% mAP increase by introducing a Historical-Aware Perception system that "remembers" the road from clear weather.

  • Hierarchical Swin-Transformer: Replaced ResNet18 with a transformer backbone to capture global contextual clues for adverse weather classification.
  • Generative Stacking: Engineered a pipeline using contrastive learning to synthesize "Snowy Night" data from clear-day imagery, solving the real-world data scarcity problem.
  • Temporal Cross-Attention: Developed a GPS-based retrieval system (using Ithaca365) that pulls a historical clear-day frame of the current coordinate to help the model "see through" current occlusion.
