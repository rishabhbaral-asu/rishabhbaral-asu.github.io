Research Computer vision, multimodal AI, LLM evaluation

Moneyball with LLMs

Tabular summarization in sports narratives

This project studies how language models transform long-form sports commentary into structured statistical output. The emphasis is on robust extraction, numerical consistency, and multi-entity reasoning.

  • Goal: convert dense narrative into accurate, structured tables.
  • Evaluation: compare models on robustness and consistency rather than surface fluency alone.
  • Outcome: accepted as a Findings paper to ACL 2026.

PHANTOM RECALL

When familiar puzzles fool smart models

A benchmark designed to reveal when a model leans on memorized answer patterns instead of recomputing the answer under a changed premise.

  • Benchmark design: puzzle variants that preserve surface form while changing the actual dependency.
  • Analysis: automatic error classification to identify failure patterns.
  • Focus: separate recognition from true reasoning.

“The Moon that Looks Like a Boat”

Evaluating visual similes in text-to-image models

This work examines whether text-to-image models can preserve the intended figurative comparison while still generating a coherent image.

  • Focus: align prompt semantics with figurative intent.
  • Problem: models often over-literalize or collapse the metaphor.
  • Direction: compare prompt styles and model responses across simile categories.

Clinical Radiographs

Unified 2D/3D multi-task framework

A clinical imaging framework that unified 2D and 3D medical image analysis across MedMNIST v2 datasets while also leveraging unlabeled MIMIC-CXR data through a student-teacher pipeline.

  • Method: dimension-agnostic design with ACS convolutions and semi-supervised learning.
  • Implementation: PyTorch, RAD-DINO, EMA, and Apple M3 optimization.
  • Result: state-of-the-art Test AUC of 0.878 on the reported benchmark setup.

Clinical Data Pipeline

DICOM parsing, segmentation, and localization

Built a medical imaging pipeline that normalized DICOM scans, supported segmentation, and enabled continuous coordinate regression for anatomical localization.

  • Pipeline: pydicom-based ingestion and normalization.
  • Models: U-Net with ResNet-18 encoder, plus regression architectures for age estimation and localization.
  • Outcome: strong validation accuracy and low localization error on the reported experiments.

GIS & Image Processing

  • Data: Landsat-8 and Sentinel-2.

  • Use case: wildfire damage and hurricane motion visualizations.

MicroCaml

Compiler architecture and functional language design

Implemented a small functional language in OCaml, spanning lexing, parsing, AST generation, and an interpreter for higher-order functions.

  • Frontend: lexer and recursive-descent parser.
  • Runtime: environment-based binding and closure-based evaluation.
  • Additional work: regex engine and tree-based database simulation.

DL from Scratch

Manual implementation of backpropagation

Built a deep learning framework in NumPy to understand training from first principles rather than relying on a high-level library alone.

  • Core layers: linear, ReLU, and softmax.
  • Optimization: Adam and SGD with momentum and weight decay.
  • Goal: derive gradients and training mechanics from the ground up.
